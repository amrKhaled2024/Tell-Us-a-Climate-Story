CO2 Emision Values for Texas ,Dallas(2000 - 2022)

In Texas, life was once defined by the steady hum of trucks on highways, oil refineries billowing smoke, and airplanes crisscrossing the sky. Day after day, CO2 emissions quietly accumulated as people went about their routines, unaware of the invisible impact on the environment. The state's energy-driven economy, particularly in cities like Houston and Dallas, contributed significantly to rising global temperatures. Climate scientists raised alarms, but the changes felt distant and gradual, almost easy to ignore.

Then, in 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic brought everything to a standstill. Lockdowns emptied roads, slowed oil production, and grounded planes. The once-lively energy of Texas faded, and in that silence, something remarkable occurred—CO2 emissions dropped dramatically. The air cleared, skies brightened, and pollution levels fell across the state. Texans, for the first time, saw how drastically their actions affected the environment. This shift was a powerful reminder that even in a state synonymous with energy production, small changes can make a big difference in reducing our carbon footprint and protecting the planet’s future.
